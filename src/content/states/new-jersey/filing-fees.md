# New Jersey Divorce Filing Fees — Complete Cost Breakdown (2026)

New Jersey's divorce filing fee is among the higher in the country — but the overall DIY cost for an uncomplicated case is manageable.

> **Disclaimer:** Fees change. Always confirm the current fee with your county's Superior Court Family Part before filing.

---

## Base Filing Fee: $300–$325

The filing fee for a Complaint for Divorce in New Jersey is set statewide and as of 2026 is approximately **$300–$325**. The amount may vary slightly by county due to local surcharges.

Call your county's Family Part Clerk before filing to confirm the exact current amount.

## Additional Court Fees

| Fee | Amount | When |
|---|---|---|
| Counterclaim fee (defendant) | ~$175–$200 | If defendant files a counterclaim |

| Certified copies of Final Judgment | $15–$25 per copy | When you pick up the Judgment |
| Transcript fees | Varies | If you need a transcript of any hearing |

## Fee Waiver

If you cannot afford the filing fee, you may apply for a **fee waiver** (Poverty Waiver). Ask the Family Part Clerk for the fee waiver application. You must demonstrate that your income is at or below the applicable threshold. The court reviews the application and may waive all or part of the fees.

**Legal Services of New Jersey** (lsnj.org) provides free legal assistance to income-qualified applicants.
