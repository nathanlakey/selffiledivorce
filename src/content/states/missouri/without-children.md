# Missouri Dissolution of Marriage Without Children (2026)

Missouri dissolution without children can be finalized quickly — as few as 6–10 weeks from filing in an agreed, uncomplicated case. No Form 14 or Parenting Plan is needed.

---

| Factor | Rule |
|---|---|
| Minimum time | 30 days from filing |
| Filing fee | $100–$163 |
| Property scope | Marital property only (non-marital property excluded) |
| Maintenance | Discretionary — 8 statutory factors |

## What the Marital Settlement Agreement Must Cover (No Children)

### All Marital Real Property

- Identify as marital (acquired during marriage) vs. non-marital (pre-marital, inherited, gift)
- For marital real estate: agreed value, equity split, who keeps or sale terms
- Refinancing deadline; fallback to forced sale
- Deed recording at county Recorder of Deeds after refinancing

### Non-Marital Real Property

- Identify the pre-marital or inherited property
- Confirm stays with original owner
- Confirm the other spouse has no claim; include waiver language
- If a mortgage exists: confirm refinancing to remove non-owner spouse

### All Marital Accounts

- Bank accounts opened or funded during the marriage → marital
- Investment accounts → identify when funded
- Retirement accounts → marital portion is what was contributed/earned during the marriage
  - Specify QDRO for employer plans; IRA transfer for IRAs
  - Specify the marital portion (e.g., "50% of the balance attributable to the period [marriage date] to [dissolution date]")

### Vehicles

- Vehicles purchased during marriage → marital → assign to one spouse; refinance out of joint loan
- Pre-marital vehicles → non-marital → generally excluded (document this)

### Debts

- Marital debts (incurred during marriage): assign; include indemnification
- Pre-marital debts: confirm responsibility remains with original debtor

### Maintenance

Missouri maintenance can be included even in a no-children dissolution if either spouse has a qualifying need. Options:
1. Maintenance order: amount, duration, termination events (death, remarriage, cohabitation)
2. **Maintenance waiver:** "Each party waives any claim for maintenance, now and in the future."

Include one or the other — do not leave it silent.

## Post-Dissolution Steps

- **Deed:** Quitclaim Deed → Recorder of Deeds in the county where the property is located
- **Vehicles:** Missouri DOR title transfer form
- **Retirement:** QDRO for employer plans after Decree is entered
- **Beneficiaries:** Update 401k, IRA, life insurance designations
- **Name change:** Social Security Administration → Missouri DOR → bank accounts
