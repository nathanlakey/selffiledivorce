# How to File for Divorce in California Without a Lawyer (2026)

California has the longest mandatory waiting period of any state — 6 months from the date your spouse is served — but also one of the best free self-help systems. California is a strict 50/50 community property state.

> **Disclaimer:** This is general legal information, not legal advice. Consult a licensed California family law attorney for your specific situation.

## California Divorce Requirements

### Residency
At least one spouse must have lived in **California for 6 months** and in the **filing county for 3 months** immediately before filing (Family Code § 2320).

### Grounds for Divorce
California is a **no-fault only** state. The sole grounds are:
- **Irreconcilable differences** — used in virtually all California divorces
- **Permanent legal incapacity to make decisions**

### The 6-Month Waiting Period
California imposes a **mandatory 6-month waiting period** from the date the respondent is served (or files a response). This is the longest mandatory wait in the country and **cannot be waived** under any circumstances (Family Code § 2339).

## California Is a Strict 50/50 Community Property State
California divides community property equally — not "equitably," but exactly 50/50. All income earned and assets acquired during the marriage belong equally to both spouses.

## Step-by-Step: California Uncontested Divorce

1. **Confirm residency** — 6 months in California, 3 months in county
2. **Get forms** from selfhelp.courts.ca.gov — FL-100 (Petition), FL-110 (Summons), FL-141 (Declaration re Service)
3. **File FL-100 + FL-110** at the Superior Court Clerk
4. **Serve your spouse** — must be served by someone 18+ who is not you
5. **Automatic Temporary Restraining Orders (ATROs)** take effect at filing — neither party can transfer assets
6. **Both spouses complete FL-150 (Income and Expense Declaration)** and FL-142 or FL-160 (asset disclosure)
7. **Wait 6 months** from service date
8. **File judgment packet** — FL-180 (Judgment), FL-170 (Declaration for Default), MSA
9. **Receive signed judgment** from the court
