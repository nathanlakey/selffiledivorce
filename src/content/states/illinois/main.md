# How to File for Divorce in Illinois Without a Lawyer (2026)

Illinois has no mandatory waiting period, a short 90-day residency requirement, and uses unique terminology: "allocation of parental responsibilities" instead of custody, and "parenting time" instead of visitation. Illinois is no-fault only.

> **Disclaimer:** This is general legal information, not legal advice. Consult a licensed Illinois family law attorney for your specific situation.

## Illinois Divorce Requirements

### Residency
At least one spouse must have been an **Illinois resident for 90 days** before filing (750 ILCS 5/401).

### Grounds for Divorce
Illinois is a **no-fault only** state since 2016. The sole ground is:
- **Irreconcilable differences** — the court may presume irreconcilable differences if the parties have lived separate and apart for 6 months, but separation is not required

### No Mandatory Waiting Period
Illinois has **no mandatory waiting period**. The divorce can be finalized as soon as all paperwork is complete and the court schedules a hearing. Cases with children typically take longer due to parenting plan requirements.

## Illinois Unique Terminology (2016 Reform)

Illinois completely reformed its family law in 2016:
- **"Allocation of parental responsibilities"** replaces "custody"
- **"Parenting time"** replaces "visitation"
- **"Decision-making responsibility"** covers education, healthcare, religion, and extracurricular activities

## Step-by-Step: Illinois Uncontested Divorce

1. **Confirm residency** — 90 days in Illinois
2. **Get forms** from illinoislegalaid.org — document assembly available
3. **File Petition for Dissolution of Marriage** at Circuit Court
4. **Serve your spouse** or have them sign an Entry of Appearance
5. **Complete financial disclosures** — both parties
6. **File Marital Settlement Agreement** and (if children) Allocation Judgment and Parenting Plan
7. **Attend final hearing** — typically brief for uncontested cases
