# Kentucky Dissolution of Marriage Filing Fees and Costs (2026)

Kentucky has **one of the lowest divorce filing fees in the country** — $113 to $148 depending on the county. Combined with free AOC forms, Kentucky is one of the most affordable states for self-represented filers.

---

## Core Filing Costs

| Fee | Amount |
|---|---|
| Circuit Court filing fee (Petition) | $113–$148 (varies by county) |
| Service fee (certified mail) | $10–$20 |
| Service fee (sheriff/process server) | $30–$75 |
| Certified copies of Decree | $5–$15 per copy |
| Deed recording at County Clerk | $13–$40 per document |

---

## County Variations

Most Kentucky counties charge approximately $113–$130 for the Circuit Court filing fee. Larger counties (Jefferson County/Louisville, Fayette County/Lexington) may charge up to $148. Always confirm the current fee with the Circuit Court Clerk before filing.

---

## Fee Waiver — In Forma Pauperis (IFP)

Kentucky waives filing fees for qualifying low-income filers.

**How to apply:**
1. Complete the Motion to Proceed In Forma Pauperis and attached Affidavit
2. File with the Petition (or before)
3. Judge reviews and approves or denies
4. If approved: filing fee waived; service fees may also be waived

**Eligibility guidelines:**
- Income at or near the federal poverty level
- Receipt of public assistance (Medicaid, SNAP, SSI, KTAP)

## Kentucky's Cost Advantage

Kentucky's $113–$148 filing fee compares favorably to other states:
- Florida: $400+
- California: $435+
- Connecticut: $350+
- Oregon: $287–$301

The AOC forms are also free at courts.ky.gov/forms — no need to purchase forms from a third-party service.
