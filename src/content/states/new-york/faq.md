# New York Divorce FAQ — 35 Most Common Questions Answered (2026)

---

## Basics

**Can I file for divorce in New York without a lawyer?**
Yes. New York's Unified Court System provides a free Uncontested Divorce Packet at nycourts.gov/courthelp specifically designed for DIY filers.

**Why does New York divorce get filed in "Supreme Court"?**
In New York's unusual court structure, the Supreme Court is a trial-level court — not the highest court. Every county has a Supreme Court, and that's where divorces are filed. The Court of Appeals is New York's highest court.

**Does New York have a waiting period?**
No mandatory waiting period. Processing takes several months in practice — especially in NYC — but there's no legal minimum.

**What are New York's residency requirements?**
You need 2 years of NY residency, OR 1 year if you were married in NY, lived here as a couple, or the grounds occurred here, OR both spouses currently live in NY and grounds occurred here.

**What are grounds for divorce in New York?**
No-fault irretrievable breakdown (used in virtually all cases) plus fault grounds (cruel treatment, abandonment, adultery, imprisonment, separation). Use irretrievable breakdown for DIY divorces.

## Timeline

**How long does a New York divorce take?**
No mandatory waiting period, but court processing means: upstate 4–8 weeks for simple uncontested cases; NYC 3–6 months for uncontested cases.

**Why does it take so long in NYC?**
Court caseloads. NYC's Matrimonial courts process thousands of cases. There's no legal delay — just administrative processing time.

**Can I speed things up?**
File a complete, error-free packet on the first submission. This is the single most impactful thing you can do. Consider filing in an upstate county if you qualify.

## Forms and Procedure

**Where do I get New York divorce forms?**
Free at nycourts.gov/courthelp — the official Uncontested Divorce Packet.

**What is the UD-4 form?**
The Sworn Statement of Removal of Barriers to Remarriage. Required if your marriage was solemnized in a religious ceremony. Addresses religious barriers (like a Jewish "get") to the other spouse's remarriage.

**How much does it cost?**
$210 index number fee + $30–$95 Note of Issue fee = $335 minimum. Among the lowest minimums of any major state.

**Do I have to go to court?**
Usually no. Most uncontested NY divorces are processed "on the papers" — the judge reviews in chambers and mails back the signed Judgment.

**What is the Statement of Net Worth?**
A comprehensive financial disclosure form both parties must complete and exchange. Can be waived by written agreement in uncontested cases.

**What is the difference between the County Clerk and the Matrimonial Clerk?**
County Clerk: where you pay the index number fee and file your initiating documents. Matrimonial Clerk: where you submit your complete divorce packet for processing. Different offices.

## Property

**Is New York a community property state?**
No. New York is an equitable distribution state — property is divided fairly, not automatically 50/50.

**What is the Majauskas formula?**
The New York standard for dividing defined benefit pension plans. Calculates the marital share based on the ratio of years of service during the marriage to total years of service.

**What about NYC co-op apartments?**
Co-op transfers require board approval and are more complex than a simple deed transfer. Contact your co-op board early in the process to understand their requirements.

**Does New York have a real property transfer tax for divorces?**
New York has a transfer tax, but transfers pursuant to divorce are generally exempt. NYC has additional transfer taxes, also generally exempt for divorce transfers.

## Children

**Does New York favor mothers in custody?**
No. New York courts apply the best interest of the child standard without gender preference.

**Can my child choose which parent to live with?**
New York courts consider the child's preference based on age and maturity. There is no binding age-based election rule — it's one factor among many.

**How is child support calculated?**
New York uses the CSSA formula: combined parental income × percentage (17% for 1 child, 25% for 2, etc.) up to the income cap ($163,000 combined as of 2025). Each parent pays their proportional share.

**What is the CSSA income cap?**
$163,000 of combined parental income as of 2025. For income above the cap, courts have discretion.

## After Divorce

**How do I change my name in New York?**
Your Judgment can include a name restoration. Take certified copies to the Social Security Administration first, then NY DMV for your driver's license.

**What if my ex violates the Judgment?**
File a motion to enforce with the Supreme Court. For child support specifically, the Support Collection Unit (SCU) can assist with enforcement.
