# How to File for Divorce in New York Without a Lawyer (2026)

New York's most confusing feature: the "Supreme Court" is actually the trial court where divorces are filed — not an appellate court. New York uses Uncontested Divorce (UD) forms and has 5 different residency pathways.

> **Disclaimer:** This is general legal information, not legal advice. Consult a licensed New York family law attorney for your specific situation.

## New York Divorce Requirements

### Residency — 5 Pathways
New York has 5 ways to meet residency (DRL § 230):
1. Either spouse was a NY resident for **2 years** before filing
2. Either spouse was a NY resident for **1 year** AND you were married in NY
3. Either spouse was a NY resident for **1 year** AND you lived in NY as a married couple
4. Either spouse was a NY resident for **1 year** AND the grounds occurred in NY
5. Both parties are NY residents on the filing date AND the grounds occurred in NY

**Most common path:** One spouse has been a NY resident for 2 years.

### Grounds for Divorce
New York allows both no-fault and fault-based grounds.

**No-fault ground (used in most cases):**
- **Irretrievable breakdown** for at least 6 months (DRL § 170(7)) — must be stated in the Verified Complaint

**Fault grounds:** Cruel and inhuman treatment, abandonment for 1+ year, imprisonment, adultery.

### No Mandatory Waiting Period
New York has **no mandatory waiting period** after filing. However, the 6-month irretrievable breakdown must have occurred before filing, and all issues must be resolved before the court will sign the judgment.

## Filing in New York — Important Notes

**Supreme Court** — Divorces are filed in the Supreme Court, which is New York's trial court (confusingly named). File at the Supreme Court Clerk's office (also called the County Clerk in most counties).

**UD Forms** — New York provides an Uncontested Divorce (UD) packet. Use the UD forms at nycourts.gov/divorce.

## Step-by-Step: New York Uncontested Divorce

1. **Confirm residency** under one of the 5 pathways
2. **Get UD forms** from nycourts.gov/divorce
3. **Complete and file** Summons with Notice (UD-1) or Summons and Verified Complaint (UD-2)
4. **Serve your spouse** — personal service by non-party 18+
5. **Spouse signs Affidavit of Defendant** (UD-7) acknowledging service
6. **Complete remaining UD forms** — Affidavit of Plaintiff (UD-6), proposed Judgment (UD-11)
7. **Submit judgment package** to the clerk
8. **Judge signs judgment** — no hearing required in most uncontested cases
