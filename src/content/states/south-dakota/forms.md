## Separation Agreement — Filed with the Complaint

South Dakota's approach is to file the **Separation Agreement simultaneously with (or shortly after) the Complaint**. This means you should have the Separation Agreement fully negotiated, signed, and notarized before filing.

**The Separation Agreement must cover:**

**Marital Real Property:**
- Full address and legal description
- Agreed fair market value; mortgage balance; marital equity
- Assignment: who keeps (buyout; refinancing deadline; fallback; Quitclaim Deed → SD Register of Deeds) or sale (proceeds split; timeline)

**Marital Financial Accounts:**
- Institution, type, balance; assignment; transfer instructions

**Retirement Accounts:**
- QDRO for employer plans (marital portion from marriage to separation)
- IRA: transfer incident to divorce
- SDRS (South Dakota Retirement System): sdrs.sd.gov — DRO after Decree

**Vehicles:** Assignment; loan assumption; SD DMV title transfer

**Marital Debts:** Creditor, balance, who assumes, indemnification

**Separate Property Acknowledgment:** Each spouse's separate property; confirmed as not subject to division

**Alimony:**
Award with amount, duration, payment terms — or explicit waiver

**If Children:**
- Legal and physical custody
- Comprehensive Parenting Plan
- Child support per South Dakota Guidelines

## South Dakota Child Support Guidelines

South Dakota uses an income shares model:
1. Determine both parents' gross monthly income
2. Apply allowed deductions
3. Look up combined obligation in SD Child Support Schedule
4. Allocate proportionally between parents by income share
5. Adjust for health insurance, childcare, parenting time

**Duration:** South Dakota child support generally continues to age **18** or through high school graduation (not to exceed 19).

---

## Alimony — South Dakota Factors (SDCL § 25-4-41)

South Dakota courts consider all relevant factors, including:
- The length of the marriage
- The parties' respective earning capacities
- The parties' ages, health, and physical condition
- The parties' financial condition and circumstances
- The station and occupation of each party
- Each party's relative fault in the divorce (fault grounds relevant)
- Contribution as homemaker
