# Wisconsin Divorce With a House — Your Options (2026)

The family home is often the most significant marital asset in a Wisconsin divorce. Wisconsin's community property framework means home equity from a marital home is presumed to be divided equally — but the Marital Settlement Agreement controls the outcome in an agreed case.

---

## Is the Home Marital Property?

**Purchased during the marriage with marital income:** Marital property — 50/50 presumption applies.

**Owned before the marriage by one spouse:** Individual property — stays with the original owner. However:
- If marital income was used to pay the mortgage during the marriage → the marital portion of equity may be subject to division
- If the home was titled jointly during the marriage → may convert to marital property

**Inherited home:** Individual property — stays with inheriting spouse.

---

## Option 1 — One Spouse Keeps the Home

**Marital Settlement Agreement must include:**
- Full address and legal description
- Agreed fair market value (appraisal or agreed estimate)
- Equity calculation: agreed value – mortgage payoff = total equity
- Each spouse's share of equity (typically 50/50; document any agreed deviation with the deviation factors)
- Buyout method: cash, offset against retirement/other assets, or deferred
- **Mandatory refinancing deadline** — keeping spouse refinances into their name alone within X days of Judgment
- **Fallback:** If refinancing fails, the home is listed for sale
- Who pays mortgage, taxes, insurance, HOA, and maintenance until refinancing
- **Deed transfer:** Leaving spouse signs Quit Claim Deed after refinancing → record at county Register of Deeds

---

### Recording the Deed in Wisconsin

After judgment and refinancing:
1. Prepare a **Quit Claim Deed** (standard for Wisconsin divorce transfers)
2. Leaving spouse signs and notarizes
3. Record at the county **Register of Deeds** in the county where the property is located
4. Pay recording fees ($30–$80)
5. No Wisconsin transfer fee applies to transfers incident to divorce — confirm with the Register of Deeds

---

## Option 2 — Sell and Split Proceeds

**Marital Settlement Agreement must include:**
- Each spouse's percentage of net proceeds (typically 50/50)
- Listing timeline after Judgment
- Agent selection and listing price method
- Who pays carrying costs during listing
- Who occupies and whether occupancy compensation applies
- Price reduction trigger and frequency
- What happens if one party refuses to sign closing documents

---

## Option 3 — Deferred Sale

One spouse stays for a defined period, then the home is sold.

**Marital Settlement Agreement must include:**
- Duration of deferral (specific date or event)
- Who pays all carrying costs
- Occupancy compensation (or explicit waiver)
- Major repair authorization and payment
- Sale process and proceeds split at end of deferral
- Default and fallback provisions

---

## Pre-Marital Home — Documentation Matters

If one spouse owned the home before the marriage:
- Document the pre-marital value (original purchase price or pre-marital appraisal)
- Track the mortgage balance as of the marriage date
- Identify what portion of mortgage paydown during the marriage came from marital income
- Marital income contributions may create a marital property interest in the appreciation or equity

In the MSA, clearly state the home's pre-marital status and agree on what (if any) marital interest was created:
> "Husband owned the home at [address] prior to the marriage. The parties agree the marital interest in the home (based on mortgage payments made from marital income) is valued at $X, which is awarded to Wife. The remainder of the equity ($Y) is Husband's individual property."
