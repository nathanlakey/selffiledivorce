# How to File for Divorce in Texas Without a Lawyer (2026)

Texas is a community property state with a 60-day waiting period and a well-established DIY divorce process supported by TexasLawHelp.org.

> **Disclaimer:** This is general legal information, not legal advice. Consult a licensed Texas family law attorney for your specific situation.

## Texas Divorce Requirements

### Residency
At least one spouse must have been a **Texas domiciliary for 6 months** and a **resident of the filing county for 90 days** before filing.

### Grounds for Divorce
Texas allows both no-fault and fault-based grounds. For DIY divorces, use **insupportability** (the no-fault ground) — simply that the marriage has become insupportable because of discord or conflict.

### The 60-Day Waiting Period
Texas requires a **mandatory 60-day waiting period** from the date the petition is filed. The divorce cannot be finalized before 60 days have passed.

## Where to File
File at the **District Court** in the county where either spouse meets the residency requirement.

## Property Division in Texas
Texas is a **community property** state. Property acquired during the marriage belongs equally to both spouses. Texas uses a "just and right" standard — not strictly 50/50, but courts start from equal division.

## Step-by-Step Process

1. Confirm residency (6 months state, 90 days county)
2. Get forms from TexasLawHelp.org
3. File Original Petition for Divorce at District Court
4. Serve your spouse (sheriff or acceptance of service)
5. Wait 60 days
6. File Final Decree of Divorce
7. Attend brief final hearing
