## The Affidavit Process (No Children)

This is Alaska's fastest and simplest divorce path:

1. Confirm Alaska domicile (no minimum duration)
2. Inventory all marital and separate property
3. Draft and finalize the **Separation Agreement**; both sign and notarize
4. Complete all forms including the **Affidavit of Uncontested Divorce** — signed by both parties
5. File all documents at Alaska Superior Court; pay $250
6. **No hearing required** — court reviews paperwork administratively
7. Decree of Divorce mailed to both parties (4–8 weeks after filing)

---

## Separation Agreement — What to Cover (No Children)

### Marital Real Property
- Legal description; agreed FMV; mortgage balance; marital equity
- Assignment: one keeps (buyout; refinancing deadline; fallback; Deed → Alaska Recorder) or sale

### Marital Financial Accounts
- Institution, type, balance; assignment; transfer

### Retirement Accounts
- QDRO for employer plans
- IRA: transfer incident to divorce
- Alaska PERS: doa.alaska.gov/drb — DRO after Decree
- Alaska TRS: doa.alaska.gov/drb

### Vehicles
- Assignment; loan assumption; Alaska DMV transfer

### Marital Debts
- Creditor, balance, who assumes, indemnification

### Alimony/Spousal Support
Award — or explicit waiver:
"Each party waives any and all claims for spousal support, now and forever."

### Separate Property Acknowledgment
State each spouse's separate property explicitly; confirmed as remaining with that spouse.

---

## Post-Divorce Steps (No Children)

- Record Deed at Alaska Recorder's Office in the correct recording district
- QDRO for employer retirement plans
- Alaska PERS or TRS DRO for state/public employees
- Update vehicle titles — Alaska DMV
- Restore name: Alaska DMV → Social Security → bank accounts
- Update all beneficiary designations immediately
