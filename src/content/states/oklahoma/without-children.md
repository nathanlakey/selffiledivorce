# Oklahoma Dissolution of Marriage Without Children (2026)

With no minor children of the marriage, Oklahoma dissolution has a **10-day** waiting period — one of the shortest in the United States. Agreed dissolutions can be finalized in as little as 3–5 weeks.

## Property Settlement Agreement — What to Include (No Children)

### All Marital Property

**Real estate:**
- Legal description from deed
- Agreed value; mortgage payoff; net equity
- Who receives the property — or sale
- Refinancing deadline (keeping spouse only)
- Fallback
- Quit Claim Deed → County Clerk recording

**Financial accounts:**
- Each account opened or funded during the marriage → assign or split
- Transfer instructions

**Retirement accounts:**
- QDRO for employer plans (marital portion from marriage date to separation date)
- IRA transfer incident to divorce

**Vehicles:**
- Assign each vehicle; one spouse assumes the loan
- Oklahoma Tax Commission title transfer

**Personal property:**
- List significant items; residual "all remaining personal property" clause

### All Marital Debts
- Each debt assigned; indemnification language: "Spouse A assumes Debt X and holds Spouse B harmless"

### Alimony
- Award (type — support or in lieu of property; amount; duration; termination events) — OR
- Waiver: "Each party waives any claim for alimony, now and forever"

### Separate Property
- Confirm each spouse's separate property; other spouse waives claim

---

## The Brief Final Hearing

Oklahoma typically requires a brief final hearing even for uncontested dissolutions without children. Both Joint Petitioners (or the Petitioner in a solo filing) appear before the District Court judge. Expect:
- 10–20 minutes
- Testimony: your name, residency, marriage date, separation, incompatibility, that the PSA was freely signed
- Judge reviews the PSA
- Judge signs the **Decree of Dissolution of Marriage**
