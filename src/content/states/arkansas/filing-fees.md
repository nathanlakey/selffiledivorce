# Arkansas Divorce Filing Fees and Costs (2026)

Arkansas charges a **uniform $165 filing fee** for a Complaint for Divorce across all 75 counties. This remarkable consistency is unusual nationally — most states have wide county-by-county variation.

---

## Court Filing Fee

| Fee | Amount |
|---|---|
| Complaint for Divorce | **$165** (all 75 Arkansas counties) |
| Counterclaim for Divorce (Respondent) | ~$100–$165 (varies) |
| Certified copies of Decree | $5–$15 per copy |
| Re-opening a closed case | Varies |

---

## Service Costs

| Method | Approximate Cost |
|---|---|
| Acceptance/Waiver of Service (spouse agrees) | Free |
| Sheriff's service | $30–$60 per service |
| Certified mail service | $10–$20 |
| Service by publication (unknown location) | $75–$200 (newspaper) |

**Tip:** For agreed divorces, having the Respondent sign an Acceptance of Service eliminates the service cost entirely.

---

## Indigency Waiver — Affidavit of Indigency

Arkansas waives the filing fee for qualifying low-income filers.

**How to apply:**
1. Complete an **Affidavit of Indigency** (available at arcourts.gov or from the Circuit Clerk)
2. File with or before the Complaint
3. If approved, the $165 fee is waived
4. Service costs may also be waived

**Eligibility:** Generally, income at or below 125% of the federal poverty level, or receipt of public assistance (SNAP, Medicaid, SSI).

## Why Arkansas's $165 Is Notable

Most states show wide variation:
- Texas: $250–$350+ depending on county
- California: $435
- Florida: $408
- Arkansas's flat $165: lower than most, and consistent

This makes Arkansas one of the more affordable states for uncontested divorces.
