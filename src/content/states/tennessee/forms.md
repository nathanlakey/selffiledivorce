# Tennessee Divorce Forms — Complete Guide (2026)

Tennessee provides standardized divorce forms at **tncourts.gov**. The most important documents in a Tennessee divorce are the Marital Dissolution Agreement (MDA) and (if applicable) the Permanent Parenting Plan (PPP).

---

## Where to Get Tennessee Forms

**Primary source:** tncourts.gov → "Forms" → "Family Law" → select the appropriate packet

Some counties maintain their own local forms or addenda. Call your county's Circuit or Chancery Court Clerk to confirm whether any local forms are required.

---

## Core Forms: Divorce Without Children

| Form | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Complaint for Divorce | Initiating document filed by Petitioner |
| Civil Cover Sheet | Required for court filing |
| Summons | Notifies Respondent of the case |
| Acceptance of Service / Waiver | Respondent's voluntary acceptance (eliminates sheriff service) |
| Marital Dissolution Agreement (MDA) | The signed settlement agreement — REQUIRED for irreconcilable differences |
| Final Decree of Divorce | The court order — signed by the judge |

## Core Forms: Divorce With Children (Add These)

| Form | Purpose |
|---|---|

| Child Support Worksheet | Calculates child support per Tennessee Child Support Guidelines |
| Final Decree of Divorce with Children | Version incorporating PPP and support order |
| Notice of Parent Education Seminar | Information about required seminar |

## The Marital Dissolution Agreement (MDA)

The MDA is the central document in a Tennessee no-fault divorce. Without it, irreconcilable differences cannot be granted.

### What the MDA Must Cover

**Real property:**
- Address and legal description of each property
- Who receives it; equity division; sale timeline if applicable
- Deed transfer instruction (to county Register of Deeds)

**Personal property:**
- Vehicles (year, make, model; who keeps each; refinancing)
- Bank and investment accounts (account numbers; split or assignment)
- Retirement accounts (plan name; QDRO or IRA transfer; division formula)
- Household goods and furnishings (general assignment or inventory)
- Business interests (if any)

**Debts:**
- Mortgage (assigned to keeping spouse or addressed in sale)
- Vehicle loans (assigned to keeping spouse; refinance required)
- Credit cards (assigned by account; indemnification)
- Student loans (treat as marital if proceeds benefited the family)

**Alimony / Spousal Support:**
Choose the applicable type — or include mutual waiver:
- **Alimony in futuro:** Long-term periodic payments; terminates on death or remarriage; modifiable
- **Alimony in solido:** Fixed lump sum or fixed installments; not modifiable; not terminated by remarriage
- **Rehabilitative alimony:** Temporary support for education/training; capped duration
- **Transitional alimony:** Short-term adjustment support; not modifiable after set period

Both spouses must sign the MDA before a notary.

## The Permanent Parenting Plan (PPP)

Tennessee requires the PPP to be on the official statutory form. The PPP must include:

- **Primary residential parent** designation
- **Residential parenting schedule:** Exact schedule for school year and summer
- **Holiday and vacation schedule:** Each named holiday and alternation pattern
- **Decision-making:** Major decisions (education, healthcare, extracurricular) — joint or primary parent
- **Parenting time to non-primary parent:** Weekly schedule, exchanges
- **Dispute resolution process**
- **Child support:** Amount, payment method, termination

Download the current PPP form from tncourts.gov.

## Deed Recording in Tennessee

Tennessee counties use the **county Register of Deeds** for recording real estate transfers.

After the divorce:
1. New deed prepared (Quitclaim Deed or Warranty Deed)
2. Leaving spouse signs and notarizes
3. Record at the county Register of Deeds where property is located
4. Fee: approximately $5–$20 per document
