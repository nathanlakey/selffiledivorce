# Massachusetts Divorce Costs and Filing Fees (2026)

Massachusetts's filing fee is moderate, but the overall cost of a Massachusetts divorce is often higher than comparable states because of court scheduling delays, mandatory hearings, and the complexity of the all-property equitable distribution standard.

---

## Core Filing Costs

| Fee | Cost |
|---|---|
| Probate and Family Court filing fee | $200–$220 |
| Certified copies of Certificate of Absolute Divorce | $10–$20 per copy |
| Service fees (1B — sheriff or constable) | $40–$80 |

## Fee Waiver (Affidavit of Indigency)

Massachusetts courts waive fees for qualifying low-income filers.

**How to apply:**
1. Complete the **Affidavit of Indigency** (available at mass.gov/forms)
2. File with your divorce petition
3. Court reviews income and assets
4. If approved: filing fees and service fees are waived

**Eligibility:** Income at or below 125% of federal poverty level, or active receipt of public assistance (MassHealth, SNAP, TAFDC, SSI, etc.).

## Note on Registry of Deeds Fees

Massachusetts recording fees are among the higher in the country. The Registry of Deeds charges a fee per page plus a base recording fee. For a standard deed transfer in a divorce:
- Prepare the deed (Quitclaim Deed is standard)
- Record at the county Registry of Deeds where property is located
- Budget $105–$175 for recording, plus deed preparation cost
