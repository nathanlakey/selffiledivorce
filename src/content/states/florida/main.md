# How to File for Divorce in Florida Without a Lawyer (2026)

Florida calls child custody "time-sharing" (not custody or visitation), requires mandatory financial disclosure, and offers a Simplified Dissolution process for qualifying couples. The 20-day waiting period is waivable by agreement.

> **Disclaimer:** This is general legal information, not legal advice. Consult a licensed Florida family law attorney for your specific situation.

## Florida Divorce Requirements

### Residency
At least one spouse must have been a **Florida resident for 6 months** before filing (Florida Statutes § 61.021).

### Grounds for Divorce
Florida is a **no-fault only** state. The sole ground is:
- **Irretrievable breakdown of the marriage** — self-certifying

### The 20-Day Waiting Period
Florida requires **20 days** after filing before a divorce can be finalized. This can be waived if the parties include a waiver provision in their Marital Settlement Agreement (Florida Statutes § 61.19).

## Florida's Unique Features

**Time-sharing** — Florida does not use the words "custody" or "visitation." The parenting schedule is called a "time-sharing schedule" and the legal framework is "parental responsibility" (shared or sole).

**Mandatory financial disclosure** — Both parties must serve a Financial Affidavit (Rule 12.285) within 45 days of service. Required in virtually all Florida divorces.

**Simplified Dissolution** — Available if: no minor children, wife is not pregnant, both agree on property division, and both appear at the final hearing. Faster and simpler process.

## Step-by-Step: Florida Uncontested Divorce

1. **Confirm residency** — 6 months in Florida
2. **Get forms** from flcourts.gov — Petition for Dissolution (12.901), Marital Settlement Agreement (12.902(f)(1))
3. **File petition** at the Circuit Court Clerk
4. **Serve your spouse** or have them sign an Answer/Waiver
5. **Both complete Financial Affidavits** within 45 days
6. **Wait 20 days** (waivable by agreement in MSA)
7. **File Final Judgment** packet
8. **Attend brief final hearing**
