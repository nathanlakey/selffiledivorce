# Nevada Divorce Filing Fees and Costs (2026)

Nevada's filing fee varies by county. Clark County (Las Vegas) and Washoe County (Reno) have slightly higher fees due to volume and infrastructure.

---

## Court Filing Fees

| Fee | Amount |
|---|---|
| Clark County (Las Vegas) | ~$320 |
| Washoe County (Reno) | ~$299 |
| Other Nevada counties | $284–$320 |
| Certified copies of Decree of Divorce | $1–$5 per page |
| Additional certified copies | Same per-page rate |

---

## Service Costs (Solo Complaint Only)

| Method | Approximate Cost |
|---|---|
| Acceptance of Service (spouse agrees) | Free |
| Clark County Sheriff | $30–$75 |
| Private process server (Clark County) | $50–$150 |
| Service by publication | $150–$300 |

**Joint Petition:** No service required — both parties already filed. Eliminates service costs entirely.

---

## Fee Waiver

Nevada courts waive filing fees for qualifying low-income filers.

**How to apply:**
1. Complete the **In Forma Pauperis Application** (available at the court or at nevadalawhelp.org)
2. File with the Complaint or Joint Petition
3. If approved: filing fee waived

**Eligibility:** Income at or below 125% of federal poverty level; receipt of public assistance; expenses that substantially exceed income.

---

## Resident Witness Affidavit — Cost

The Resident Witness Affidavit itself is free (obtain the form from the court or nevadalawhelp.org). Notarization of the affidavit: typically $5–$25 at a bank, UPS Store, or notary service.
