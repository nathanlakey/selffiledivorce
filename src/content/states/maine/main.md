# How to File for Divorce in Maine Without a Lawyer (2026)

Maine makes self-represented divorce straightforward: one of the lowest filing fees in the country, no waiting period, no minimum residency requirement, and an accessible court self-help system backed by excellent free resources from Pine Tree Legal Assistance.

> **$120 filing fee — one of the lowest in the US:** Maine's $120 filing fee is among the lowest of any state for a divorce petition.

> **No waiting period:** Maine imposes no mandatory waiting period after filing. The divorce can be finalized as soon as the court processes the paperwork and schedules a hearing.

> **Current resident — no minimum:** You only need to be a current Maine resident. There is no minimum length of time you must have lived here before filing.

> **"Irreconcilable marital differences" — the no-fault ground:** Maine's no-fault ground. No fault or misconduct needs to be proven.

> **Parent education program — required with children:** When minor children are involved, both parents must complete a court-approved parent education program before the divorce is finalized.

> **Financial Statement — required:** Both parties must complete and file a Financial Statement disclosing income, expenses, assets, and debts.

## No Waiting Period — Notable

Maine is one of the few states with no mandatory waiting period between filing and finalization. Once the paperwork is filed, the Respondent is served, and any parent education requirements are satisfied, the case can move directly to a final hearing. For agreed cases, this means finalization can occur in weeks rather than months.

---

## No Minimum Residency — File Now

If Maine is your current residence, you can file today. Maine does not impose a 6-month, 90-day, or any other minimum residency requirement. This makes Maine particularly accessible for people who have recently moved to the state.

---

## Financial Statement — Mandatory

Both parties must complete and file a **Financial Statement** disclosing:
- Monthly income (gross and net)
- Monthly living expenses
- All assets (real property, bank accounts, retirement, vehicles, personal property)
- All liabilities (mortgage, car loans, credit cards, other debts)

The Financial Statement is used by the District Court to evaluate spousal support requests and equitable distribution of marital property.

## Step-by-Step Overview

### Step 1 — Confirm Residence in Maine
You currently live in Maine. No minimum time required.

### Step 2 — Inventory Marital Property
Maine uses equitable distribution. Gather all financial documentation.

### Step 3 — Draft the Divorce Agreement / Marital Settlement Agreement
Address all property, debts, spousal support, and child-related matters.

### Step 4 — Complete Financial Statements
Both parties complete and sign their Financial Statements.

### Step 5 — File at District Court
File in the county where either spouse lives. Pay $120.

### Step 6 — Serve the Respondent
Serve the Complaint and Summons — or obtain a signed Acceptance of Service.

### Step 7 — Parent Education Program (If Children)
Both parents complete the court-approved program. File certificates with the District Court.

### Step 8 — No Waiting Period
No mandatory delay. Schedule the final hearing.

### Step 9 — Final Hearing
Judge reviews Financial Statements, Divorce Agreement, and Parenting Plan. Divorce Decree entered.

### Step 10 — Post-Divorce Steps
Record deeds at Maine Registry of Deeds. QDRO for retirement plans. Update titles, accounts, beneficiaries.
