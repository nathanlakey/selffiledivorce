# How to File for Divorce in Georgia Without a Lawyer (2026)

Georgia has no mandatory waiting period and some of the lowest filing fees among large states. Georgia also has an excellent free DIY divorce portal at diy.georgiacourts.gov. Divorces are filed in the defendant's county.

> **Disclaimer:** This is general legal information, not legal advice. Consult a licensed Georgia family law attorney for your specific situation.

## Georgia Divorce Requirements

### Residency
At least one spouse must have been a **Georgia resident for 6 months** before filing (O.C.G.A. § 19-5-2).

**File in the defendant's county** — Georgia divorces are filed in the Superior Court of the county where the respondent (non-filing spouse) lives, not the petitioner.

### Grounds for Divorce
Georgia allows both no-fault and fault-based grounds.

**No-fault ground:**
- **Irretrievable breakdown** — the marriage is irretrievably broken (O.C.G.A. § 19-5-3(13))

**Fault grounds (13 total):** Adultery, desertion, cruel treatment, habitual drunkenness, drug addiction, mental incapacity, impotency, force/menace/duress/fraud at marriage, pregnancy by another man at time of marriage, conviction of felony, incurable mental illness.

### No Mandatory Waiting Period
Georgia has **no mandatory post-filing waiting period**. However, the respondent has 30 days to file an Answer after being served. Uncontested divorces typically finalize 4–8 weeks after service.

## Georgia's Unique Features

**File in defendant's county** — file where your spouse lives, not where you live. Exception: if your spouse is a non-resident, file in your own county.

**DIY portal** — diy.georgiacourts.gov generates completed Georgia divorce forms based on your answers.

**Child age 14 rule** — children 14 and older can express a preference for which parent they live with, and the court gives significant weight to that preference.

## Step-by-Step: Georgia Uncontested Divorce

1. **Confirm residency** — 6 months in Georgia
2. **Use the DIY portal** at diy.georgiacourts.gov to generate your forms
3. **File at the Superior Court** in your spouse's county
4. **Serve your spouse** — sheriff service or Acknowledgment of Service (free if spouse cooperates)
5. **Wait 31 days** after service (30-day response period + 1 day)
6. **File Final Judgment and Decree of Divorce** with your Settlement Agreement
7. **Brief hearing or on-the-papers** depending on your county
